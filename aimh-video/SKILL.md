# AIMH Video

## Overview

The audience is not buying the product — they're buying **how you think**. Every
build is raw material for a short video about solving real problems: why you chose
this, how you decided, what you learned. This skill helps you (a) notice when a
moment is worth filming and (b) shape it into a tight 3–5 minute story.

**Core rule:** capture the *decision*, not the feature. "Watch me add a button" is
boring. "Here's how I killed a feature I loved, and the rule that made me" is a video.

## When a moment is film-worthy

Flag it the instant any of these happen — proactively say
**"📹 This looks like a video moment: <one line>"** and offer to outline it:

- A **verdict**: an idea moves to Building or Archived (especially a kill).
- A **decision under tension**: you chose the cheap test over building; cut scope; said no.
- A **surprise**: the riskiest assumption was wrong; the data contradicted your gut.
- A **before/after**: a workflow that was painful is now one step.
- A **principle in action**: a decision-skills rule (default-not-to-build, kill criteria, compliments≠demand) visibly changed what you did.
- A **shipped artifact**: something now exists that didn't this morning.

Not film-worthy: routine edits, refactors, config, "it works now" with no decision behind it.

## The 3–5 minute arc

Five beats. Time-box the whole thing — if it doesn't fit in 5 minutes, cut, don't rush.

1. **Hook (0:00–0:15)** — the tension or result, stated cold. No "hey guys."
2. **Problem (0:15–1:00)** — the specific painful before-state. One real user/moment.
3. **How I decided (1:00–2:30)** — the *thinking*: the framework, the riskiest assumption, the cheapest test, the kill criteria. This is the actual content.
4. **What I built / did (2:30–4:00)** — show the artifact; keep it tight, screen + voice.
5. **Result & learning (4:00–5:00)** — what the signal said, the verdict, and the one transferable lesson. End on the lesson, not "like and subscribe."

## Hook formulas

- "I spent three days building this — then deleted half of it. Here's why."
- "Everyone says [X]. I tested it for $0 in two days. They're wrong."
- "This is the question that kills most of my ideas before I write any code."
- Contrarian claim · surprising number · the kill · the before/after jump-cut.

## B-roll & screens

- Screen-capture the *decision surface* (the kill criteria, the score, the board move) — not just the UI.
- A whiteboard/notebook shot of the framework beats trying to narrate it abstractly.
- One jump-cut: the painful "before" vs. the one-step "after."

## Pitch deck (5 slides, if you want one)

1. **The problem** — one sentence, one user.
2. **Why now** — what changed that makes this worth doing today.
3. **The bet** — riskiest assumption + the cheapest test of it.
4. **The result** — the signal, honestly (including nulls).
5. **The lesson** — the transferable thinking. This is what people remember.

## Scope guard

One idea per video. One lesson. Under 5 minutes. Showing your *reasoning* honestly —
including the things that didn't work — beats a polished demo every time.
